Talent landscape analysis · Theorema

Bioinformatics + AI: where the people actually are, and how to reach them.

This is a working document, not a proposal. I've spent the past days mapping the talent landscape behind the role Filip outlined — bioinformatik s AI zkušenostmi, těch je jak šafránu. What follows are three hypotheses, a category- and geo-level map of where candidates actually sit, a read on reachability, and a proposed process. The goal is to validate — or break — this thinking together before we commit to an approach.

Prepared by: Roman Csutkai For: Filip Doušek, Theorema Scope: Sourcing strategy for Data Analyst + R&D Forensics agent teams
01

The problem, stated clearly

Theorema's "Data Analyst" and "R&D Forensics" are not two distinct roles — they are two flavours of a single archetype: mid-level computational biologists who have lived inside messy, multi-source biological data, plus adjacent ML-agent engineers with scientific domain fluency. That reframing matters, because it changes the sourcing strategy from "two parallel searches" to "one search, two emphases."

The hire in one sentence. Python-first. 5–10 years post-training. Has built pipelines, not just used them. Comfortable with omics data, assays, and LLM-agent plumbing. English working language. Builder mindset over report-writer mindset.

Why this is hard — specifically

The pool is tiny in CEE

"Bioinformatician with AI experience" is single-digit-per-country in Central Europe. This is not a LinkedIn-search problem. The signal lives in GitHub contributor graphs, middle-authorships on method papers, and preprint servers — not in "Open to Work" badges.

Two products, one archetype

The "R&D Forensics" framing is metaphorical — it is about reasoning from fragmentary biological evidence (terminated programs, negative datasets), not criminal DNA work. But actual forensic geneticists reason probabilistically from degraded inputs every day. Their skill transfers directly, and two globally rare diasporas are on the market right now.

Seed-stage optionality vs. pharma comp

A senior computational biologist at Novartis, Roche, or BioNTech earns what Theorema likely cannot match in cash. The comp conversation will be harder than the skill conversation. Equity + mission + autonomy must do real work here.

The reachability signal is subtle

The strongest "ready to move" signal is a preprint whose author affiliation changes between versions. The second strongest is end-of-contract timing at a core facility. LinkedIn activity is a lagging indicator at best.

Three hypotheses I'd like to validate with you

  1. Archetype is one, not two. Both products draw from the same candidate pool, with different emphasis on "builds pipelines" vs. "reasons from fragmentary evidence." If this is wrong, the sourcing approach changes materially.
  2. Forensic-genetics transferability is real, not a stretch. People who've worked on mixture deconvolution, probabilistic genotyping, and DNA phenotyping have the exact skill Theorema's R&D Forensics product needs — and two diasporas (Kayser circle in NL, USC Santiago unit in ES) are uniquely accessible right now.
  3. Prague + seed equity is sellable to this archetype — provided we lead with mission and problem substrate, not base-salary comparison. Especially for the Italian, Polish, and Indian cohorts where CEE mobility is structurally high.
02

Candidate profile composition

The composite. The target candidate holds a PhD or senior MSc in computational biology, bioinformatics, computational chemistry, or an adjacent discipline that combines molecular biology with strong computer science. They are 5–10 years post-training, Python-first, SQL-adjacent, and fluent in the modern bioinformatics tooling stack — nf-core, Snakemake, Nextflow, scverse. They have domain fluency in at least one of: omics, pharma R&D, proteomics, pathogen genomics, or forensic-style probabilistic inference. They speak working English.

Three archetype sub-profiles

A

The Pipeline Builder

Staff scientist or senior bioinformatician at a core facility or academic lab. Already ships code. Lives in GitHub. Typical location: CEITEC, NBIS, EMBL, Pasteur Hub, NNF-CPR, Sanger. Highest in-count.

B

The Pharma Refugee

Mid-career R&D scientist at Big Pharma or biotech who has seen a failed program. Knows what negative data looks like. Frustrated with slow cycles, layered reviews, and 18-month decisions. Typical location: Roche, Novartis, BI, Idorsia (post-layoff), BioNTech, Selvita.

C

The Forensic Transferable

Forensic geneticist or population-genetics researcher who reasons probabilistically from degraded inputs. Narrow, globally rare, high-fit for R&D Forensics. Typical location: Parson lab Innsbruck, USC Santiago, Copenhagen, Kayser diaspora.

Where candidates sit today

Three rings describe the recruitable geography. Each ring has a different relocation cost profile and therefore a different pitch.

1

One flight from Prague — CZ, SK, AT, DE-S, PL, HU The cluster play. ELIXIR-connected, English-working, Prague-relocatable within a single flight or overnight train. Permanent under-supply of startup optionality among mid-career scientists.

2

EU + UK — 2-flight tolerance The depth pool. Sanger, EMBL-EBI, Pasteur Hub, Human Technopole, NBIS, NNF-CPR, Hartwig. Requires a real relocation case — the pitch shifts to mission and technical substrate.

3

Global diasporas worth activating Kayser circle (post-Rotterdam closure), USC Santiago forensic genetics, Israel, India via EU Blue Card. Narrow, high-fit, and currently live for reasons specific to each.

04

Reachability — who can actually be moved, and when

Three signals, read together, tell us a candidate is reachable. A named individual with a public channel (email, GitHub, LinkedIn). A life-phase fit — end-of-contract, diaspora from a recent reorganisation, post-layoff window. And a pull signal — Prague proximity, language match, or startup-optionality appetite. What follows splits the map by present-moment reachability.

High · time-sensitive windows Act now

  • Kayser diaspora. Erasmus MC Dept of Genetic Identification closed 2025. Vidaki, Ralf, Simons, Xiong now distributed. Globally rare skill on the open market.
  • Idorsia post-layoffs (2023–24). Swiss pharma comp-bio in forced mobility. Short-window opportunity before absorption by Basel peers.
  • NBIS / SciLifeLab. ~90 bioinformaticians on rolling 1–4yr contracts. Predictable churn — monitor the quarterly ending-cohort.
  • Institut Pasteur Hub. ~40 engineers on CDD contracts. English-fluent. International-rotation-native. Natural pool for Prague.
  • Human Technopole Milan. Italians on assegno contracts. Unusually Prague-open for cultural and salary-parity reasons.

Medium · worth activating Build warm

  • CEITEC + BIOCEV + IOCB + IMG. Brno/Prague cluster. Hometown advantage. Quarterly on-site coffees beat any job board.
  • Vienna BioCenter. Shortest EU hop. CeMM (Bock, Menche), IMBA, IMP, MedUni AI institute.
  • IIMCB / CeNT / MCB. Warsaw and Kraków. Dense comp-bio and structural-bio talent. Prague is both practical and aspirational.
  • USC Santiago Forensic Genetics. Lareu, Phillips, Carracedo unit. Deepest forensic-phenotyping pool globally. Post-ISFG 2024 visibility.
  • Hartwig / UMC Utrecht. Clinical WGS operators already rotating out to startups.

Lower · know about, don't chase Context

  • Sotio Biotech Prague. Direct peer competitor. Expensive poach, counter-offers guaranteed. Track for referrals, not placements.
  • Civil-service forensic labs. BKA Wiesbaden, NFI The Hague, Institute of Criminalistics Prague. Structural immobility — advisory relationships only.
  • US top-tier academic. Broad, MIT, Stanford. Prague relocation unlikely without existing EU tie. Useful for diaspora identification.
  • China / Japan / Korea. Low relocation probability unless already a diaspora candidate. Useful for remote-contractor or advisory relationships.
05

Proposed process

Four phases. Each phase has a clear output and a decision gate. The point of validating before executing is to avoid three months of sourcing against the wrong profile.

1

Validate

Confirm the archetype hypothesis, comp band, and the forensic-transferability thesis. Output: role spec document both sides sign off on.

2

Plan Strategy

Select channels by ring and category. Prioritise time-sensitive windows. Seed the OpenAlex/GitHub graph from Theorema's likely tool stack. Output: sourcing plan with named targets and channel-specific tactics.

3

Execute

Three channels in parallel: CEE cluster play, forensic-transferable diaspora outreach, OpenAlex+GitHub graph refresh. Output: weekly candidate report — name, channel, state, fit read.

4

Refine

Continuous feedback loop — each rejection refines the archetype definition. Monthly graph refresh surfaces new preprint-mobility signal. Output: living 200–400 person shortlist that compounds across hiring cycles.

What I'd want to validate first. The three hypotheses in Section 1. Whether the forensic-transferability play lands with you or feels like a stretch. And whether the comp band Theorema can realistically offer matches the archetype we're describing — because if it doesn't, we reshape before we sprint, not during.
