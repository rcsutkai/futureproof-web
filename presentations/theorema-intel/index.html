Talent landscape analysis · Theorema

Bioinformatics + AI: where the people actually are, and how to reach them.

This is a working document, not a proposal. I've spent the past days mapping the talent landscape behind the role Filip outlined — bioinformatik s AI zkušenostmi, těch je jak šafránu. What follows are three hypotheses, a category- and geo-level map of where candidates actually sit, a read on reachability, and a proposed process. The goal is to validate — or break — this thinking together before we commit to an approach.

Prepared by: Roman Csutkai For: Filip Doušek, Theorema Scope: Sourcing strategy for Data Analyst + R&D Forensics agent teams
01

The problem, stated clearly

Theorema's "Data Analyst" and "R&D Forensics" are not two distinct roles — they are two flavours of a single archetype: mid-level computational biologists who have lived inside messy, multi-source biological data, plus adjacent ML-agent engineers with scientific domain fluency.

The hire in one sentence. Python-first. 5–10 years post-training. Has built pipelines, not just used them. Comfortable with omics data, assays, and LLM-agent plumbing. English working language. Builder mindset over report-writer mindset.

Why this is hard — specifically

The pool is tiny in CEE

"Bioinformatician with AI experience" is single-digit-per-country in Central Europe. The signal lives in GitHub contributor graphs, middle-authorships on method papers, and preprint servers.

Two products, one archetype

The "R&D Forensics" framing is metaphorical. But actual forensic geneticists reason probabilistically from degraded inputs every day. Their skill transfers directly, and two globally rare diasporas are on the market right now.

Seed-stage optionality vs. pharma comp

A senior computational biologist at Novartis, Roche, or BioNTech earns what Theorema likely cannot match in cash. Equity + mission + autonomy must do real work here.

The reachability signal is subtle

The strongest "ready to move" signal is a preprint whose author affiliation changes between versions. LinkedIn activity is a lagging indicator at best.

Three hypotheses I'd like to validate with you

  1. Archetype is one, not two. Both products draw from the same candidate pool.
  2. Forensic-genetics transferability is real, not a stretch. Two diasporas (Kayser circle in NL, USC Santiago unit in ES) are uniquely accessible right now.
  3. Prague + seed equity is sellable to this archetype — provided we lead with mission and problem substrate.
02

Candidate profile composition

The target candidate holds a PhD or senior MSc in computational biology or adjacent discipline. They are 5–10 years post-training, Python-first, SQL-adjacent, fluent in nf-core, Snakemake, Nextflow, scverse.

Three archetype sub-profiles

A

The Pipeline Builder

Staff scientist or senior bioinformatician at a core facility. Already ships code. Lives in GitHub. Typical: CEITEC, NBIS, EMBL, Pasteur Hub. Highest in-count.

B

The Pharma Refugee

Mid-career R&D scientist who has seen a failed program. Frustrated with slow cycles. Typical: Roche, Novartis, BI, Idorsia (post-layoff), BioNTech, Selvita.

C

The Forensic Transferable

Forensic geneticist who reasons probabilistically from degraded inputs. Narrow, globally rare, high-fit for R&D Forensics. Typical: Parson lab Innsbruck, USC Santiago, Kayser diaspora.

04

Reachability

High · time-sensitive Act now

  • Kayser diaspora. Erasmus MC closed 2025. Globally rare skill on market.
  • Idorsia post-layoffs. Swiss pharma comp-bio in forced mobility.
  • NBIS / SciLifeLab. ~90 bioinformaticians on rolling contracts. Predictable churn.
  • Institut Pasteur Hub. ~40 engineers on CDD contracts. International-rotation-native.
  • Human Technopole Milan. Italians on assegno contracts. Prague-open.

Medium · worth activating Build warm

  • CEITEC + BIOCEV + IOCB + IMG. Brno/Prague cluster. Hometown advantage.
  • Vienna BioCenter. Shortest EU hop. CeMM, IMBA, IMP.
  • IIMCB / CeNT / MCB. Warsaw and Kraków. Prague is both practical and aspirational.
  • USC Santiago Forensic Genetics. Deepest forensic-phenotyping pool globally.
  • Hartwig / UMC Utrecht. Clinical WGS operators rotating to startups.

Lower · know about Context

  • Sotio Biotech Prague. Direct peer. Track for referrals, not placements.
  • Civil-service forensic labs. Structural immobility — advisory only.
  • US top-tier academic. Prague relocation unlikely without existing EU tie.
  • China / Japan / Korea. Low relocation probability unless diaspora.

Proposed process

1

Validate

Confirm archetype hypothesis, comp band, forensic-transferability thesis. Output: role spec both sides sign off on.

2

Plan Strategy

Select channels by ring and category. Prioritise time-sensitive windows. Output: sourcing plan with named targets.

3

Execute

Three channels in parallel: CEE cluster, forensic diaspora outreach, OpenAlex+GitHub graph. Output: weekly candidate report.

4

Refine

Continuous feedback loop. Monthly graph refresh. Output: living 200–400 person shortlist that compounds across hiring cycles.
