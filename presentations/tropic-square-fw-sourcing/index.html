01 · The Role

What we understand about this position

Drawn from the job specification and Tropic Square’s public roadmap. No filler.

  • Mission. Lead the transition from silicon-validation firmware to production-grade firmware for TROPIC01 — the world’s first open-source, auditable secure element, now shipping in the Trezor Safe 7 at volume. The engineering priority has shifted: production firmware, not validation code.
  • Technical stack. Primary languages: C and Python targeting ARM and RISC-V microarchitectures. Bare-metal and RTOS mastery required. Secure boot, cryptographic hardware engines (ECC, TRNG), driver architecture, and hardware–software co-design.
  • Seniority & team. Senior IC. Leads a small, agile team of low-level developers. Defines deployment and testing environments. Architects secure firmware from first principles — not inheriting a locked-down corporate stack.
  • Security depth required. Firmware-level defences against side-channel power analysis, electromagnetic emanation, and fault injection — the attacks TROPIC01’s physical design resists, and which the firmware layer must not undo.
  • Long-term mandate. Evaluate and drive migration of key firmware components to Rust. Open architectural decision — the incoming engineer owns the timeline and the direction. Prague-based, SatoshiLabs financial backing.

    First Touch — Mission Alignment, Not a Recruitment Pitch

    • Outreach reads as an invitation to collaborate on an open-source movement. SatoshiLabs pedigree and TROPIC01’s position as the world’s first open, auditable secure element in a shipping product is the hook.
    • Subject line: “Defining production-level firmware for TROPIC01 / SatoshiLabs”
    • Reference the candidate’s specific public work in the first two sentences — their GitHub contribution, their paper, their conference talk. No template-only messages.
    • Offer an informal, peer-to-peer discussion with the engineering lead — not an HR screen.
    • The open-source credentials are concrete: RTL is public. Firmware is public. Threat model is public.

    Socratic Qualification — Technical Peer, Not HR Filter

    • Conducted with Tropic Square engineering lead present. Non-negotiable: elite candidates disqualify on HR-only screens.
    • Architectural: “How would you design a reproducible firmware build and test environment for an open-source secure element from scratch, and transition code safely from silicon-validation to production reliability?”
    • Security: “When designing driver code for cryptographic engines like ECC, what firmware-level defences do you implement to prevent side-channel power analysis or electromagnetic emanation?”
    • Toolchain: “In your experience with RISC-V or ARM, how do you handle custom compiler toolchain configurations, and what are the trade-offs between RTOS and bare-metal?”
    • Leadership: “How do you guide a specialised development team to maintain consistent standards while fostering the creative freedom needed for open-source R&D?”

    Operational Integration — Address Structural Needs

    • Confirm hybrid work arrangement and flexible hours in Prague. This is a product company, not remote-first.
    • For relocation candidates: initiate the relocation playbook in parallel with technical qualification. Pre-qualify visa pathway before advancing to offer.
    • Introduce to the engineering team early — quality of conversation sells the role for Archetypes B and C.
    • Confirm Rust autonomy concretely: “You define the migration timeline and the decision architecture. C is primary today.”
    • Present competitive regional base salary alongside equity structure and growth roadmap.

    Close — Archetype-Specific Levers

    • Select the archetype tab below to see specific closing tactics by profile type.
    • Counter to “it’s a startup; too risky”: “TROPIC01 is in volume production in the Trezor Safe 7. The financial backer is SatoshiLabs, profitable since 2013. The question isn’t if this chip exists — it’s whether you want to define its firmware.”
    • Counter to “salary doesn’t match”: walk through cost-of-living comparison; confirm equity and sign-on flexibility.
    • Counter to “I don’t know Rust”: “C is the primary language. Rust migration is a roadmap item — you own the timeline and the decision architecture.”
    • Counter to “I need a visa”: “We handle the full immigration case: Employee Card or Blue Card, all documentation. Timeline is 60–90 days.”
    • Not looking now: add to nurture sequence; send TROPIC01 milestone announcements. The right moment comes — don’t close the loop.

    By candidate archetype

    Closing levers unique to TROPIC01

    Equity in Tropic Square

    TROPIC01 is in volume production. Even a modest ESOP with 4-year vest / 1-year cliff represents meaningful asymmetric upside if the company scales to broader IoT and security chip markets.

    Name on the Founding Firmware

    TROPIC01 v1.0 production firmware has no lead author yet. The incoming engineer defines and ships that work. In 5 years, every hire pitch in this space will reference having built TROPIC01’s production stack.

    Conference Speaking

    Tropic Square has active incentive to present at CHES, RWC, DEF CON HHV, and Embedded World. An engineer who builds the firmware is the natural speaker. Career-defining exposure NXP or Bosch cannot match.

    Rust Autonomy

    The Rust migration decision is explicitly open. The incoming engineer owns it. A rare opportunity to define the language direction for a production secure-element firmware stack — referenced in the Rust-embedded community for years.
