01 — The Role

The role as we understand it

Five dimensions from the job specification and company context. Nothing invented.

  • Technical Stack Primary languages C and Python targeting ARM and RISC-V microarchitectures. Bare-metal programming, RTOS, secure boot, cryptographic hardware integration (ECC, HSM), custom compiler toolchains. Rust experience valued; Rust migration assessment is an explicit long-term mandate.
  • Seniority & Scope Senior individual contributor transitioning into team lead. Will lead a small, agile team of low-level developers. Defines deployment and testing environments. Architects secure firmware for production silicon — not validation or prototype work.
  • Mission TROPIC01 is the world’s first open-source security chip — now shipping in the Trezor Safe 7. The incoming engineer defines production firmware from the ground up for silicon that has just entered global volume production. A founding contribution, not a maintenance role.
  • Team & Org Small team of low-level developers within Tropic Square s.r.o., Prague — a fabless semiconductor company founded by the SatoshiLabs / Trezor team. Financial backing from SatoshiLabs (profitable since 2013). On-site Prague; hybrid working available.
  • Rust Mandate Assess and drive the migration of key firmware components to Rust for future product lines. The engineer owns this decision — timeline, architecture, and rationale are theirs to define. This is not a Rust job; it is a “should we go Rust and how” mandate.

    06 — Candidate Journey

    Knock to close — the full pipeline

    Four phases, five archetypes, four TROPIC01-specific closing levers. Everything calibrated to the actual candidate population.

    High-tech mission alignment — not a cold call

    The initial message reads as an invitation to collaborate on an open-source movement, not a recruitment pitch. Lead with TROPIC01’s transparent architecture, the Trezor Safe 7 deployment, and SatoshiLabs financial credibility. Reference the candidate’s specific open-source contribution or paper in the first two sentences.

    • Subject: “Defining production-level firmware for TROPIC01 / SatoshiLabs”
    • Contrast closed-source NDA environments with TROPIC01’s fully auditable, public-RTL silicon
    • Position the role as defining production firmware from scratch — not inheriting a legacy stack
    • Close with a peer-to-peer framing: “informal discussion with the engineering lead in Prague”

    Socratic qualification — led by technical peers, not HR

    Elite engineers are highly sensitive to automated filtering and LeetCode-style tests. The screening process must be led by technical peers using Socratic questions that evaluate structural and architectural decision-making.

    • “How would you design a robust firmware build and test environment for an open-source secure element from scratch?”
    • “When designing driver code for cryptographic engines like ECC, what firmware-level defences do you implement against side-channel power analysis?”
    • “In your experience with RISC-V or ARM, what are the primary trade-offs when evaluating RTOS versus bare-metal for a security-critical system?”
    • “How do you guide a specialised team to maintain engineering standards while fostering the creative freedom needed for open-source R&D?”

    Operational integration — remove structural blockers early

    Before advancing to offer, pre-qualify three vectors in parallel to avoid late-stage collapse on non-technical grounds.

    • Hybrid work arrangement and Prague practicalities confirmed — not an afterthought
    • Relocation pathway initiated for non-CEE candidates: EU Blue Card (CZK 73,823/month threshold from May 2026) or Czech Employee Card; full case management, 30–60 day processing target
    • Compensation framing: Prague market rate (€55–80k) + cost-of-living reset + equity + sign-on; purchasing power parity comparison shared proactively
    • Partner employment: Prague English-language tech market is strong — addressed proactively, not reactively

    Close — archetype-specific, not generic

    Each archetype has a distinct closing tactic detailed below. Universal TROPIC01 levers: equity in a company with proven commercial traction; name on the founding firmware team; conference speaking budget; R&D autonomy on the Rust migration decision. Pre-built counters for the six most common rejection patterns are in the research report.

    • Counter “startup risk”: TROPIC01 is in volume production in Trezor Safe 7 — the question is not whether this chip exists, it is whether they want to define its firmware
    • Counter “salary delta”: total package including equity and sign-on; cost-of-living comparison; purchasing power parity
    • Counter “visa complexity”: full immigration case managed end-to-end; 30–60 day timeline; bridging arrangement available if needed
    • Counter “waiting for FAANG offer”: “FAANG offers a larger team. Tropic Square offers your name on the only open-source production secure element in the world. Only one of those exists.”
